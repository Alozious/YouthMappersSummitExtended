About the Summit

The YouthMappers Leadership Fellowship Summit in Jamaica 2023 was a transformative moment, uniting global youth mappers and professionals in open mapping and geospatial data. It's a catalyst for change, sparking our ambition to host a parallel summit in Uganda. This replication aims to empower students who missed Jamaica, sharing vital skills and knowledge, fostering global connections.

Our Ugandan summit bridges knowledge gaps by exposing young individuals to cutting-edge tools and geospatial open mapping. Our goal: equip the next generation of mappers with skills to drive positive change. As post-summit enrichment can be challenging, centralizing the event enhances inclusivity. This united approach encourages all youth mappers to participate, fostering collaboration and shared growth.

From the impactful Jamaica summit to the upcoming Ugandan extension, unity and shared growth remain at the core. We're dedicated to equipping youth mappers with tools, knowledge, and a collaborative spirit. Centralizing in Uganda ensures accessibility for all, transcending geographical barriers. Together, we'll continue our legacy of positive change on a global scale.

The Main Objective

The Main Objective

The primary objective of the Extended YouthMappers Leadership Fellowship Summit in Uganda is to create a platform for knowledge sharing, capacity building, and networking opportunities for young mappers.

By extending the vision of the main summit in Jamaica, this fellowship aims to empower students who were unable to participate in the previous event due to various constraints. It seeks to inspire and equip the youth with essential skills, knowledge, and leadership abilities, enabling them to drive positive change within their communities and beyond.

Summit Themes

1.Mapping for Climate Resilience

Recognizing the urgent need to address the challenges posed by climate change, the summit will focus on the role of mapping technologies in enhancing climate resilience. Participants will explore innovative mapping approaches, tools, and strategies to mitigate the impact of climate-related disasters and promote sustainable development.

2.Activating Youth Leadership

The summit aims to foster youth leadership by providing a platform for young mappers to showcase their skills, projects, and initiatives. It will feature interactive workshops, panel discussions, and mentorship programs, empowering participants to become effective leaders in their respective fields and communities.

3.Promoting Equity and Inclusion

In line with YouthMappers' commitment to inclusivity, the summit will prioritize discussions on equity and inclusion within the mapping community. Participants will explore ways to ensure equal access to mapping technologies, address gender disparities, and promote diversity in the field of geospatial sciences.
